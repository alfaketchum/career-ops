## Design Philosophy

Career-ops is a power tool that should feel like a consumer app. The user is in
a high-stakes, emotionally charged process (job hunting). The interface must feel
like a co-pilot that knows what to do next -- not a spreadsheet that waits for
input. Every screen should answer: "What should I do right now?"

**Coinbase DNA.** Clean white content surfaces, maximum blue surface area on
navigation and hero zones, pill-shaped interactive elements, guided next-step
patterns. The blue is not decorative -- it is structural. It says "this is where
the system lives." White cards say "this is where your data lives."

**Full Voltage means:** saturated primary blue used boldly on large surfaces
(header banner, hero stat bar), not sprinkled as tiny accents. The blue is loud
and confident. Content areas stay clean white to let data breathe.

**Spatial logic:** The hero banner and stat bar are dense and packed -- information

gaps between them (Gestalt proximity -- cards that relate sit close). Section
breaks use 32-40px gaps to create clear chapter divisions.

## Layout Architecture

### Header / Hero Banner
- **Full-width blue banner** (#0055FF background, white text)
- Contains: logomark (white square with lightning icon), app title, tagline
- Baked-in stat row: 3-4 key numbers (Total Jobs, Applied, Interview, Avg Score)
  displayed inline within the banner -- not separate stat cards
- Stats use large bold numbers (32px) with small labels (11px) beneath
- The banner IS the stats. No separate "stats section" below.

### Navigation
- **Pill-shaped tabs** sitting inside or just below the hero banner
- White text on blue when inside banner, or blue on white pill when below
- Active state: solid white pill with blue text (inverted)
- Hover: semi-transparent white overlay

### Suggested Next Step
- **Compact horizontal bar** immediately below the nav pills
- Light blue surface (#E8F0FF) with a one-line prompt:
  "Next: Review 3 new matches from today's scan" [Go ->]
- NOT a tall card. NOT a hero section. A single horizontal strip, inline, 40-48px tall.
- The [Go] action is a small pill button, primary blue.

### Content Area
- **White background**, max-width 1200px, centered
- Cards are white with subtle border (NEUTRAL_200) -- no shadows by default
- One card per logical group (funnel, priorities, recent activity)
- Tables and data grids use the full card width

### Cards
- White surface, 1px border (NEUTRAL_200), RADIUS_MEDIUM
- Section titles: 13px uppercase, NEUTRAL_500, 0.06em tracking
- No colored borders. No gradient backgrounds. No elevation changes on hover.
- Hover: background shifts to SURFACE_SUNKEN (#F6F7F9) -- subtle, flat

### Blue Logomark
- 32x32px square with RADIUS_SMALL, PRIMARY background
- White lightning bolt icon centered inside
- Sits in the top-left of the hero banner
- This is the only "icon in a colored container" allowed -- it is the brand mark,
  not a decorative pattern

## Component Patterns

### Score Badges
- Pill-shaped (RADIUS_PILL)
- SCORE_HIGH: green text on green/10 background
- SCORE_MID: warning text on warning/10 background
- SCORE_LOW: error text on error/10 background
- Font: FONT_MONO, 13px, weight 600

### Status Tags
- Pill-shaped (RADIUS_PILL)
- Each status gets ONE color, applied as text + 10% opacity background
- Evaluated: PRIMARY blue
- Applied: SUCCESS green
- Interview: #7C3AED purple (exception -- interviews are special)
- Offer: SUCCESS green, bold
- Rejected: ERROR red
- Discarded/SKIP: NEUTRAL_500

### Filter Pills
- Row of pill buttons below section titles
- Inactive: NEUTRAL_100 bg, NEUTRAL_500 text
- Active: PRIMARY bg, white text
- Hover (inactive): NEUTRAL_200 bg

### Tables
- Clean horizontal lines only (NEUTRAL_200 bottom border per row)
- Header row: NEUTRAL_500 text, 11px uppercase, sticky
- Row hover: SURFACE_SUNKEN background
- No vertical borders. No alternating row colors.

### Buttons
- Primary: PRIMARY bg, white text, RADIUS_PILL, 500 weight
- Primary hover: PRIMARY_HOVER bg
- Secondary: NEUTRAL_100 bg, NEUTRAL_700 text, RADIUS_PILL
- Secondary hover: NEUTRAL_200 bg
- All buttons 36px height (compact dashboard density)

### Funnel Bars
- Background track: NEUTRAL_100
- Fill: PRIMARY (#0055FF) for all bars -- monochrome funnel
- Height: 12px, RADIUS_SMALL
- No multi-color rainbow. One color. The label differentiates, not the bar color.

### Empty States
- Centered text, NEUTRAL_500
- One line of guidance: "Run a scan to populate your pipeline"
- Optional: small PRIMARY pill button as CTA

## Motion

```
MOTION_INTENSITY: low
EASING:           cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.1, 0.25, 1)  (ease-out natural)
DURATION_FAST:    120ms  (hover states, toggles)
DURATION_NORMAL:  200ms  (tab switches, reveals)
DURATION_SLOW:    350ms  (page transitions, modals)
```

**Motion rules:**
- Hover state transitions: background-color 120ms ease
- Tab content: opacity 200ms ease (no slide, no bounce)
- No entrance animations on data cards or table rows
- No staggered reveals on repeated elements
- prefers-reduced-motion: collapse all durations to 0ms

**What is NOT animated:**
- Individual stat numbers
- Card appearances
- Table row entrances
- Score badge changes

## Interaction Patterns

### Guided UX Philosophy
The dashboard is not a passive data viewer. It actively guides the user:

1. **Suggested Next Step bar** -- always visible, always relevant
   - Pulls from: unreviewed scan results > pending evaluations > follow-up reminders
   - Single most important action, stated as a verb: "Review", "Evaluate", "Follow up"

2. **Priority ordering** -- top priorities list ranks by actionability, not just score
   - Hot leads (high score + recently posted) float to top
   - Stale items (no action in 7+ days) get flagged

3. **Progressive disclosure** -- Overview shows summary, click through for detail
   - Funnel shows counts, click a bar to filter the jobs table
   - Score badges link to full evaluation reports

### Keyboard Shortcuts
- R: refresh data
- 1-7: jump to tab by number
- /: focus search (when search is added)

## Constraints

- NO JavaScript frameworks -- vanilla JS only (existing architecture)
- NO build step -- static files served directly
- NO external CDN dependencies at runtime (fonts loaded from Google Fonts link tag is acceptable)
- MUST work in a single HTML + CSS + JS file set
- MUST remain functional at 375px mobile width
- The server is read-only -- dashboard displays data, does not mutate it

## Anti-Slop Commitments

These patterns are BANNED from this project:

1. NO gradient backgrounds on any card or data surface
2. NO indigo/violet/purple as primary (we use #0055FF -- pure blue)
3. NO colored left/top border accents on cards
4. NO icons in colored circle containers (except the one brand logomark)
5. NO hover:-translate-y on anything
6. NO staggered entrance animations on data elements
7. NO bounce or elastic easing
8. NO dark sidebar (the entire app is light mode with blue header)
9. NO pure black (#000) or pure gray -- all neutrals tinted to hue 260
10. NO transition: all -- specify properties explicitly

## Domain Competitors Studied

1. **Huntr** -- job tracker with kanban + metrics. Clean white UI, blue accents,
   card-based layout. Our hero banner approach is bolder than Huntr's flat nav.
2. **Teal** -- AI resume builder + job tracker. Uses blue primary, guided UX
   with "next steps" prompts. We adopt their guidance pattern but with more
   visual punch (full-bleed blue banner vs their subtle top bar).
3. **Coinbase** -- not a job tool, but the UX DNA: bold blue surfaces, pill nav,
   clean white content, consumer-grade clarity on complex data. This is the
   energy and polish level we are targeting.

## What Makes This Unforgettable

The **full-bleed blue hero banner with baked-in stats** is the signature. Most
dashboards put stats in separate cards below a thin nav bar. We merge the header,
stats, and navigation into one bold blue zone that takes up the top 180-200px of
the viewport. When you open career-ops, you see your numbers FIRST, in white on
electric blue. It looks like a fintech app, not a job spreadsheet.

The contrast between the bold blue chrome and the clean white data area creates
a two-tone identity that is instantly recognizable.
