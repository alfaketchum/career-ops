# Article Digest & Proof Points — Ajay Shah

## Career Arc (Detailed)

### Rutgers (2013-2018)
- Knew from day one: finance major, fascinated by banking system since 2008/09 Great Recession
- Financed own way through university with part-time jobs
- **Rutgers Economic Society:** Led team of 4 to 2nd place in macro econ forecasting competition
- **Rutgers Entrepreneurial Society:** 3-day no-sleep hackathon, built "CurateME" (subscription platform connecting local businesses with local artists), placed 2nd
- These two experiences were formative for leadership and stepping out of comfort zone

### Johnson & Johnson (2016-2017)
- Secured full-time co-op/consulting position as junior
- Manager was impressed with analytical capabilities and ability to "manage up"
- Within 6 months, advanced from supporting $300M budget to owning $10M budget on new team
- **Offered J&J's prestigious FLDP program** (Finance Leadership Development Program) at end of contract
- **Turned it down** — wanted financial services/markets career, not corporate finance track
- This was a hard decision: J&J FLDP is one of the most competitive F500 programs, sets people up for senior leadership

### Moody's Investor Service (2018-2022)
- Recruited specifically for research after turning down J&J FLDP
- Credit associate → promoted to Senior Associate (2020)
- Covered portfolio of 12 credits: property-casualty insurers, asset managers
- **High-stakes stakeholder management:** Interfaced with C-Suite at BlackRock, Fidelity; handled calls from hedge funds questioning ratings
- **Rosetta Stone Project (STAR Story):**
  - Context: Annual portfolio review required manually comparing 80-100 companies on Excel, taking ~2 weeks
  - Action: Realized IT was building new BI/data management tool. Proposed integration with Excel using call functions
  - Result: Shipped integration in weeks, saved 100+ hours/year of analyst work. Won Culture of Excellence award
  - Insight: "Out-of-box thinking and taking initiative, despite being a first-year analyst, could create change"
- **Stablecoins Interest (2020):**
  - Grew interest in fintech/stablecoins while at Moody's
  - Co-authored "Demystifying Stablecoins" report — cross-team collaboration between banking team, asset management team (Ajay's), and analytics team
  - Gave presentation to broader FIG team on Web3/Digital Assets impact on financial system
  - This was a collaboration story: "No one person can know everything"
- **Side hustle:** Built network in Web3 through freelance blogging and aggressive networking on Twitter and throughout NYC

### Aligned Co. / Alignment Engine (2022)
- **Headhunted by Sam Cassat**, CEO, who was impressed by: research analyst background, freelance blog, extensive Web3 network
- Consulting portfolio manager role — moved from theoretical (Moody's) to hands-on capital management
- Team had diverse backgrounds: electrical engineer (risk-averse, focused on technical risk), trained pianist/marketer (relationship-focused, less risk-aware)
- Ajay learned to appreciate different perspectives: "These were their strengths — one deeply thinking about technical risk, one developing key relationships"
- **Ethical dilemma story:**
  - Colleague didn't report an OTC transaction showing a 60% markdown on an investment
  - Ajay felt clients should know, even if "just one transaction"
  - Weighed options: tell clients directly vs. tell boss
  - Chose to escalate to boss, who relayed successfully. Clients thanked them for transparency
  - Lesson: "Always err on the side of transparency and caution"
- **FTX collapse (Sept 2022):** Industry crisis forced Aligned to restructure, entire investing team let go

### Limitless Labs (2023-2024)
- Met co-founder at conference in LA, first in-person meeting was at UCLA's Connection Lab (blockchain club)
- Built permissionless margin trading platform using idle liquidity
- **Fundraising (STAR Story):**
  - Persuaded Bangkok-based VC fund to lead round, tapped network for angel investors
  - Raised $500K @ $10M valuation
  - Had to overcome public speaking weakness — significant improvement through repeated pitch deck presentations
  - Also leveraged YK Pek, Singaporean restructuring lawyer, who helped set up company and found investors
- Expanded team from 2 to 5, built influencer marketing program, created strategic partnerships
- Shipped product (testnet May 2023, mainnet Nov 2023), generated ~$65K ARR
- **Departure:** Co-founder had different vision for end-game. After thoughtful discussion, Ajay opted for buyout arrangement
- **Failure story from Moody's (used in MBA prep):**
  - Time-sensitive credit rating, recently assigned company
  - Assumed existing model inputs were correct without verifying
  - Client was shocked by leverage calculation during presentation
  - Lesson: "Double check assumptions before presenting, especially in time-sensitive settings"

### Sophon (2025-Present)
- Business Strategy Manager for consumer-focused L2 blockchain
- Sourced and incubated 20+ projects, distributed $2M+ in grants/capital to ecosystem teams
- Key skills: negotiation, strategy, sourcing, ecosystem building

1. **Persuasion:** At Aligned, had to present cogent investment cases to get client greenlight on complex strategies. At Limitless, persuaded investors on innovative technology.
2. **Innovation:** Rosetta Stone at Moody's, Limitless product launch
3. **Teamwork:** Produced credit ratings, research, and innovation collaboratively across startups and large organizations

### Weaknesses (Self-identified)

2. **Organization:** Addressed through technology. At Aligned, noticed team using single Telegram group for multiple topics. Suggested switch to Discord, saw marked improvement.

## Key Narrative Frames

### The Chameleon Narrative
Non-linear path is intentional breadth: corporate finance → credit analysis → portfolio management → startup founding → ecosystem building. Each move driven by curiosity and wanting to understand how things actually work.

### The "Both Sides" Advantage
Has been on both sides of the table: evaluating companies (Moody's) and building them (Limitless, Sophon). Rating instruments and managing the capital those instruments represent.

### The Transition Story
Finance professional who made a "soft transition" to fintech/technology. Thread: Great Recession fascination → finance degree → credit analysis → stablecoins interest → DeFi portfolio mgmt → DeFi startup → blockchain ecosystem building.

### Why Stable Role Now
After startup life, values the structure, mentorship, and collaboration of being part of a team. "The best ideas came from working alongside smart people, not in isolation." Wants to fund runway for Sophon while having professional stability.
