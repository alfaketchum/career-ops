## Essay 2: Difficult Decision / Values / Leadership (Kellogg)

### The Ryan Decision (STAR Story — NEW)
- **Situation:** Spring 2023, Limitless Labs. Small team (2 co-founders + 2 junior devs + 1 designer). Six-week deadline for product demo to secure pre-seed funding.
- **Task:** Running low on seed capital. Had to reallocate resources to meet demo deadline.
- **Difficult decision:** Let go of Ryan (designer) after just one month. His role was crucial — responsible for entire UI.
- **Action:**
  - Proposed that Ajay + one junior dev step into UI design role (dev had some baseline experience)
  - Co-founder resisted: (1) emotionally challenged by letting go of core member, (2) felt they needed all hands on code, not UI
  - Ajay persuaded: "As co-founders and leaders, we have to make tough decisions. Polished UI on core product > full-fledged product for securing funding."
  - Ajay and junior dev took crash course in Figma — 2 weeks of intensive learning (buttons, layout spacing)
  - Next 3 weeks: mocked up and created functioning UI
- **Result:** Secured $500K funding at product demo
- **Values:** Collaboration, adaptability, flexibility while maintaining focus on overarching objectives
- **Leadership insight:** In startups, uncertainty is constant. Being responsive to challenges while staying focused on the goal.

**Note:** This is a strong STAR story not in the interview prep — adds: resource constraint decision-making, self-teaching (Figma), persuading resistant co-founder, delivering under pressure.
