# Duke MBA Essay — Ajay Shah

## Personal Story / 25 Random Things

### Favorite Memory
Songkran Water Festival in Chiang Mai, Thailand (2018). City transformed into 3-day monsoon — everyone young/old, poor/rich partaking. Embraced childhood-like wonder.

### Background
- Raised by single mother in Jersey City, NJ with older brother
- Jersey City "filled the gap of fatherhood" — learned street smarts in urban environment marked by crime and poverty
- Indian heritage — favorite dessert is Falooda (rose-flavored drink)
- Mother supported family through real estate — business and investing always cherished in family

### Martial Arts Journey
- 13: Taekwondo
- 14: Trained alongside Puerto Rican champions at stepfather's boxing gym in Queens, NY
- 16: First job — teaching kickboxing to 100+ students
- 20s: Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu for 5+ years — favorite sport for emphasis on strategic thinking

### Intellectual Curiosity
- Age 8: First "intelligent question" about purchasing power parity after cousin imported goods from London
- Post-2008/09: Went to Zuccotti Park for Occupy Wall Street. Realized protesters were emotionally charged but couldn't explain the Great Recession. Compelled to major in Finance.
- Admires Ben Thompson (Aggregation Theory) — how modern tech companies are unique through distribution channels and economies of scale

### Health & Philosophy
- Age 27: Made first million through investing in financial markets
- Survived 14-hour skull surgery due to rare bone condition. Grueling recovery with complications.
- Learned: "Money just solves money problems; at the end of it, you still have all your other problems" — Naval Ravikant
- Since then: prioritizes exercise, nutrition, mental health above all else
- Grounded by study and practice of Zen
- Material things are less important
